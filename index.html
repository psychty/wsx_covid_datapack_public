West Sussex COVID-19 Weekly data supplement

This pack brings together information relating to COVID-19 in West Sussex.

West Sussex County Council Public Health Department monitors information on a daily basis, and summary packs will be published weekly. Links are provided to public data sources available, with a summary of current sources available below.

Local authorities currently have access to some information that is not in the public domain, which may be due to small numbers or due to data being provisional.

The landscape of available data is changing all the time, and we hope to be able to publish as much as possible.

For more information please contact publichealth@westsussex.gov.uk

This page works best in certain browsers such as Chrome, Firefox, and Safari, and does not currently work well in Internet Explorer or Edge.

This analysis has been created by Dr Rich Tyler at the West Sussex Public Health and Social Research Unit.

Daily case changes and initial peaks

The figure below shows the daily confirmed cases of Covid-19 over time by area (showing West Sussex by default), but you can change this to any lower tier area within the county. Lower tier authority cases

The next set of figures shows a quick glance of the lower tier local authorities within West Sussex.

The black lines (shown on the actual numbers figures only) on each figure represent the rolling average number of new cases confirmed in the previous seven days.

As with other figures, the most recent five days are to be considered incomplete. As such, these dates are excluded from the calculation of change in confirmed cases.

Colour key for plots

Hover over a bar to see the number of new cases and cumulative cases to date.

The next table and visualisation shows the most recently recorded confirmed cases and the number of new confirmed cases by day from pillar 1 (NHS and PHE laboratories) and pillar 2 (commercial testing) combined. You can toggle between viewing the coloured tiles as actual confirmed cases or as confirmed cases per 100,000 population (crude, all age population as at 2018).

Given the differences in population sizes across West Sussex, you might naturally expect there to be more cases in one area than another. Viewing the cases per 100,000 allows you to compare across areas as if they had the same population size.

The cumulative cases are taken from the most recently available date, although number of confirmed cases in a single day (a proxy for new cases) is taken from six days prior (latest complete date).

A Surge in cases can in part be due to the amount of testing done locally from one day to the next. We do not currently have information on the total number of tests being conducted locally.

The colour key for the tiles has been chosen to capture the breadth of new cases across the region, whilst being sensitive to small numbers as well as periods of no reported cases.
