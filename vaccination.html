Vaccinations in England

The COVID-19 vaccination programme began in England on the 8th December 2020.

To begin with vaccinations were administered in NHS hospital hubs although from the 14th December, vaccinations were also administered through local vaccination services such as GP practices and then in care homes from the 21st December. In the new year (from 11th January 2021) Vaccination Centres such as sports stadiums also started providing vaccinations.

Vaccine supplies are growing as fast as possible as are the local centres which are delivering them. However, it is anticipated that it will take until summer/autumn months of 2021 before all adults have been offered a COVID-19 vaccine.

The data in this section come from NHS England and are collected through the National Immunisation Management Service (NIMS). It is updated every week, on a Thursday, with data up to the previous Sunday. As of 17th March 2021, all adults aged 50 and over are eligible for their first vaccination appointment.

As more age groups become eligibe for receiving the vaccine, NHS England will provide more information on vaccine uptake among younger age groups. Currently though, data is only broken down into smaller age groups for those aged 50 and over.

The Joint Committee on Vaccination and Immunisation (JCVI) advise the government on the groups that should be prioritised for vaccination. JCVI advises that the first priorities for the vaccination programme should be the prevention of COVID-19 mortality and protecting health and social care staff and systems. Secondary priorities include vaccination of those at increased risk of hospitalisation and at increased risk of exposure, and to maintain resilience in essential public services.

On 26th February 2020, the JVCI advised that an age based population strategy should be adopted for further rolling out the vaccine programme without prioirtising new occupation groups.

For more information on the UK vaccination programme see the UK Government website.

* This excludes a small number of individuals where age was unknown at the time of vaccination. Currently, those aged under 16 are not eligible for the vaccine and so this cohort has been removed from the denominator (the population at large) in calculating the proportion value.

It should be noted that we do not know exactly how many people have been offered a vaccine or how many are eligible (e.g. some health and social care staff of any age are eligible, or those who are clinically vulnerable) and so cannot say if uptake is better or worse in some areas than others.

Some areas have more older people and care homes and so these areas will likely show as having more people with vaccinations as these populations were prioritised to receive.

Using population estimates can help to understand coverage in the population. However, this does not help us to say how many people have had the vaccine, who are currently eligible for it, which will vary from area to area.

The UK does not have a population register. We have used a denominator provided by the NIMS service for estimating the population in each area. These are our best guess at the number of people in the population, using sources such as GP practice registers and it is possible that they do not capture groups of people who are not registered with GPs including people who are homeless.

Vaccine uptake by age group

The weekly publication of vaccination data by NHS England includes broad age groups. Currently, whilst those in younger age groups are not yet eligible, anyone aged under 50 years old are grouped together. Over time, as more age groups become eligible for the vaccination, it is expected that more groups will be available.

The figure below shows the number of individuals (in the orange bars) who have received at least one dose of a COVID-19 vaccine in West Sussex by age group. This figure also includes (in the dark grey bars) the number of people who have yet to receive their vaccination based on the population estimates from the NIMS service. This show that among older age groups, there are relatively fewer people yet to receive their vaccination compared to younger groups.

This is to be expected as not all age groups are eligible to receive the vaccine yet but the figure gives some idea of the progress to date and how many more people there are to offer a vaccination to.
